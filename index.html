My Artsitic Journey

I started out as a kid, drawing all the time on spare paper, particularly in class. There were tons of people who could really draw and I admired them; I even tried copying them. After a while I started to do some studying and picked up on some very useful tricks, though back then I still used standard pencil and lined paper. I made my way to the eight grade and drastically increased in skill compared to my usual work. In the spirit of that improvement I started my first comic (as competition for a comic my friend was popular for.) I was still very far from where I am now and didn't know how to make my art digital yet.

Freshman year started and I met a girl in my chinese class named Melissa. She was brilliantly talented and drew almost effortlessly. No doubt, I admired Melissa's skils more than the skills of my mother, who I targeted my goals of surpassing. I asked her for help when I could and got lots of good tips and tricks. I changed my style a second time and my training began. I switched to copy paper and only used mechanical pencils. I started looking up tutorials and I even had a rival named Porfirio who I used to call "Nauno". After 9th grade year I stuck to my training and I improved well.

I began using G.I.M.P. as a basic tool for my digital artwork. Digital art was an occasion for me, since I always found it terribly hard to pull off. I got an accout on deviantart after choosing my official artist tag; "Drahcir" (pronounced drah-sih-ur) which is my name spelled backwards and to add flair I added the roman numeral two to the end. I had everything I needed except a logo. I spent two hours trying to come up with one and finally I found it (I'm keeping the story behind it secret XD!). I started hanging out in the web design teacher's room at my high school and I began to learn how to use photoshop. My digital art began during my 11th grade year.

Today, I hang out with other talented people at school as I perfect my drawing skills and become the best I can at what I do My signature tools are my Kuru Toga mech pencil and my Wacom Intuos Pen Drawing Tablet.
